# Hansol's Notion Space — Setup Guide

This guide explains how to set up Notion as the living chronicle and planning tool for Hansol. The master framework lives in this repository; Notion is where the day-to-day life gets recorded.

---

## Why Notion

- Works on phone (Mom can add a quick note after school; Dad can add from Korea)
- Handles photos, text, tables, and databases in one place
- Hansol can eventually have her own login and contribute
- Easy to search and browse years later
- Free for personal use

---

## Who Has Access

| Person | Role |
|---|---|
| Dad | Admin — can edit everything |
| Mom | Editor — adds to the chronicle day-to-day |
| Hansol (when ready) | Contributor — has her own planning space |

Set permissions at the page level. Hansol's planning pages can be hers to manage; the family chronicle is shared.

---

## Top-Level Structure

Create a top-level page called **Hansol** with the following sub-pages:

```
Hansol/
  About Hansol          ← living profile snapshot
  Chronicle             ← the ongoing life record
  Development Tracks    ← English, Math, Arts, Independence
  Reading List          ← books database
  Planning              ← Hansol's own space
  Family Notes          ← observations from Mom and Dad
```

## Page by Page

---

### About Hansol

A simple page that gets updated once or twice a year. Copy the "Who You Are Right Now" snapshot from the framework and keep it current here.

Include:
- Photo (update it each year)
- Age, school, year
- Current strengths and interests
- What she's working on

---

### Chronicle

This is the heart of the Notion space. It's a **database** (use Notion's Gallery or Timeline view) where each entry is a moment in her life.

**Create a database with these properties:**

| Property | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Title | Text | Short description of the moment |
| Date | Date | When it happened |
| Category | Select | School / Family / Friends / Travel / Performance / Milestone / Struggle / Random |
| Who added | Person | Mom, Dad, or Hansol |
| Photos | Files | Up to a few images |
| Notes | Text | The story — what happened, what she said, what it felt like |

**Views to create:**
- **Gallery view** — visual, great for browsing by photo
- **Timeline view** — see her story unfold chronologically
- **Filter by Category** — pull up just performances, or just travel, etc.

**What to add here:**
- School reports and scorecards (photo or scan)
- Musical performances, concerts, events
- Trips and new experiences
- Moments with friends (no need for full names — just "S, her best friend since Grade 4")
- Hard moments — arguments, failures, disappointments — and how they resolved
- Random things she loved: a meal, a song, something she said
- FaceTime moments worth remembering

**Cadence:** Don't make it a chore. One or two entries a week is plenty. Better occasional and real than frequent and hollow.

### Development Tracks

One sub-page per track. Each page mirrors the framework but is the *living* version — updated with actual progress.

#### English Track Page

Include:
- Current reading level / assessment (update termly)
- **Books Read** — link to the Reading List database, filtered to read books
- **Vocabulary log** — a simple table: Word | Meaning | Date learned | Used in a sentence
- **Writing samples** — paste or photograph paragraphs she writes over time
- Notes on progress from Mom's observations

#### Math Track Page

Include:
- Current grade/level
- School report extracts (termly)
- Concepts she's mastered (checklist format)
- Interesting problems she solved or struggled with
- Links or notes on any extra resources being used

#### Performing Arts Track Page

Include:
- List of performances (role, show, year)
- Photos and videos from shows
- Skills she's building (singing, stage presence, memory)
- Her own thoughts on performing (ask her: "What did you love about the musical?")

#### Independence Track Page

Include:
- Plans she made herself (link to Planning section)
- Decisions she navigated
- Things she took responsibility for
- Observations: is she asking for help less? Making better choices?

### Planning

This is **Hansol's space**. She owns it.

Create sub-pages for each season or year. Start with:

#### Summer 2026 Plan

A page Hansol fills in herself (with light guidance). Use a simple template:

```
What I want to learn this summer:

Books I want to read:

Something I want to try that I've never done:

Something I want to get better at:

How I want to spend my mornings:

One thing I want to do with Mom:

One thing I want to do with Dad:

My goal by the end of summer:
```

After summer, add a **"How it went"** section. This closes the loop and builds the habit of reflection.

#### Future planning pages

- Term goals (each school term)
- A "things I want to try someday" list — she adds to it whenever she wants
- A "things I've learned about myself" page — starts small, grows over time

---

### Family Notes

A private place for Mom and Dad to write observations — things noticed but not ready to act on, questions to discuss, reflections after visits or calls.

This is **not** shared with Hansol yet. It's a place to think.

Suggested entries:
- "Noticed she was quieter this week — worth checking in on"
- "She mentioned wanting to learn guitar — flag for later"
- "Math seems to be clicking more. Keep momentum."
- Notes after monthly visits

## Keeping It Alive

The most important thing is that this doesn't become a project that gets abandoned.

A few principles:

**Low friction over perfection.** A one-line entry is better than a blank week. Mom can add a photo with two words. That's enough.

**Quarterly review.** Once every few months, Dad and Mom look at the chronicle together (a quick 20-minute call) and ask: what's working? What needs attention? Anything to update in the framework?

**Annual update.** Once a year, update the "About Hansol" snapshot and review each Development Track. Note what changed.

**Hansol's growing role.** Start by showing her the Reading List and the Planning page. Let her add her own thoughts to books she finishes. Gradually invite her into more of it as she gets older. By 13–14, she should be co-authoring her own story here.

---

## Notion Setup Steps (Quick Start)

1. Create a free Notion account at notion.so
2. Create a new page: **Hansol**
3. Create the six sub-pages listed above
4. Set up the Chronicle and Reading List as full-page databases
5. Invite Mom as an editor (share the top-level Hansol page)
6. Add the starter Reading List entries
7. Start the Summer 2026 Plan and sit down with Hansol to fill it in together
8. Add the first Chronicle entry: something from this week
