## The Four Development Tracks

These are four areas we're paying close attention to. Not pressure — attention. We believe in building on strengths, not fixing weaknesses.

---

### Track 1 — English

**Where you are:** Strong speaker and communicator. Vocabulary is growing.

**Where we're headed:** Reading and writing with confidence and joy. Eventually: reading books, articles, even research papers on topics you care about.

**Why it matters:** Stories are how we understand the world. The more you can read and write, the more worlds you can enter.

**The path:**
1. One new word a week — learn it, use it, own it
2. Chapter books — start thin, go longer as confidence grows
3. After each book: write one paragraph about what you thought. Not a summary — your opinion.
4. By 12: a short essay on something you care about
5. Eventually: read non-fiction books about ideas, science, the future

**Reading list:** See the curated list in the Notion space. Start anywhere that feels interesting.

**Summer 2026 target:** Read 5 books. Write a short paragraph after each one — just what you thought.

---

### Track 2 — Math

**Where you are:** Strong foundations. Good logical thinking.

**Where we're headed:** Comfortable enough with numbers to understand how the world is measured, analyzed, and predicted. Eventually: reading articles about data, statistics, and how AI works.

**Why it matters:** The future will be shaped by people who understand data. You don't have to become a mathematician — but you should feel at home with numbers.

**The path:**
1. Right now: Master the fundamentals with curiosity, not just correctness
2. By 12–13: Start thinking about *why* numbers tell stories (data, graphs, patterns)
3. By 14–15: Basic statistics — averages, distributions, probability
4. By 16–17: Read a simple paper or article about AI or statistics and understand the main idea
5. Long game: Be the person in the room who isn't afraid of the numbers

---

### Track 3 — Performing Arts

**Where you are:** You got on stage. You had a role in a musical. That's not nothing — that's courage.

**Where we're headed:** Wherever you want to take it.

**Why it matters:** Performing teaches you to express yourself, to inhabit other perspectives, to hold a room. These skills will serve you regardless of what you do.

**What we'll do:**
- Keep saying yes to opportunities to perform
- Explore singing more if it feels right
- Notice what it feels like to be on stage — that feeling is data about who you are

This track doesn't have rigid milestones. It has open doors. Walk through the ones that call to you.

---

### Track 4 — Independence

**Where you are:** Old enough to start making real choices. Smart enough to reflect on them.

**Where we're headed:** A person who can plan her own life, manage her own time, and take responsibility for her own decisions.

**Why it matters:** The best thing we can give you isn't a plan for your life. It's the ability to make one yourself.

**How we'll build it:**
- You plan parts of your own schedule — starting with summer
- When decisions come up, we'll talk through the options together rather than just telling you what to do
- We'll encourage you to try things, reflect on them, and tell us what you learned
- Mistakes are part of the curriculum

**Starting this summer:** The Summer 2026 Plan (see below) has space for you to fill in. It's yours.

## Finding Your Path

You are 10. You don't need to know what you want to do with your life.

What you need is exposure — to ideas, stories, people, and experiences that help you understand what energizes you and what doesn't.

We'll guide you mostly through **books**. Not textbooks — books about people who built things, discovered things, created things, questioned things.

When you finish a book and think "I want to know more about that," pay attention. That's a signal.

When you finish a book and feel nothing, that's information too.

Over time, you'll start to see a pattern in what lights you up. That pattern is the beginning of a path.

We'll keep an updated reading list in Notion — books chosen with you in mind. You can pick from it, suggest additions, and mark off what you've read.

## Principles We Try to Live By

These are not rules. They're more like compass directions.

**Curiosity over certainty.** The best question is better than the easy answer.

**Effort over outcome.** We care more about how hard you tried than what grade you got.

**Kindness as strength.** The fact that you slow down for your friends — that matters more than most things.

**Language as a superpower.** Every language you speak opens a new world. Protect and grow them all.

**Distance doesn't mean absent.** Your dad calls every day. Your mom is there every morning. You are loved from many directions.

**Your life is yours.** We'll guide, suggest, and occasionally push. But we are raising you toward your own choices, not ours.
